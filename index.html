I am a political scientist and climate policy expert with the Centre for Sustainability at Hertie School in Berlin, Germany. Before joining Hertie, I have been a Visiting Assistant in Research at Yale University, a doctoral researcher at at Freie Universit√§t Berlin, and an MPhil candidate at the University of Oxford.

My work focuses on political opinion formation, policy onset, and policy feedback, mostly in the field of climate change politics. Here, I frequently apply quantitative techniques such as causal inference and machine learning and often take a cross-national comparative perspective.

In my PhD thesis, I have analyzed the formation of climate change attitudes across different countries using a quantitaive anaysis of global survey data and qualitative process-tracing based on fieldwork in Germany, South Africa, and Argentina.

My research has been published in peer-reviewed journals such as Environmental Politics, Global Environmental Politics or Energy Research and Social Science. Besides academic research, I also write policy papers and consult political decision-makers and other stakeholders.
